Ingredients

Yield: 1 Charlotte

Prep Time: 20min

Refrigeration Time: 1h

Total Time: 1h20min

  • Frozen Raspberry 500g
  • Fromage Blanc/Greek Yoghurt 500g
  • Boudoir/Ladyfinger Biscuit approx. 30 large

Directions

  1. Before starting to make the charlotte, make sure to let the frozen raspberries defrost, and drain out any excess juice. You may use fresh raspberries instead if you prefer.
  2. Find a cylindrical dish to make an appropriate mould for your charlotte. A deep round glass dish works well for this.
  3. Place the ladyfinger biscuits vertically around the sides of the dish, making sure they are tightly next to one another. The sugar-coated side should face outwards.
  4. Place biscuits in the bottom of the dish, facing down. You may need to cut some to the right shape in order to fill in the whole bottom.
  5. Spoon a layer of greek yoghurt into the bottom dish. Then, add a layer of raspberries, and another layer of greek yoghurt.
  6. Add a second layer of biscuits, again cutting them into shape if needed. Then repeat the previous layers (greek yoghurt, raspberries, greek yoghurt).
  7. The layers should fill the entire dish. Add a final layer of biscuits to seal everything in.
  8. Place the dish in the fridge with a plate or cover on top. The cover should press down the charlotte so that in maintains it's shape once out of the mould.
  9. After at least an hour in the fridge, flip the charlotte onto a plate. Once the mould is removed, it is ready to serve!
  10. Enjoy!

Tip!


You may need more or less biscuits and layers depending on the size of your dish.

The biscuits are sweet, but if you want to add more sweetness, you can add sugar to the greek yoghurt or raspberries before assembling.

You can use different fillings instead of raspberries, such as canned pears, canned pinneaple or other kinds of berries.
