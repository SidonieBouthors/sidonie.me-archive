Ingredients

Yield: 8 Pita

Prep Time: 20min

Cooking Time: 40min

Wait Time: 2h30min

Total Time: 3h30min

  • Flour 3 cups / 375g
  • Olive Oil 1.5 tbsp
  • Water 1 cups / 240ml
  • Dry Yeast 1 packet / 2.5 tsp
  • Salt 2.5 tsp

Directions

  1. Warm your water in the microwave (it should be lukewarm to the touch, not too hot).
  2. In a bowl, add the yeast to the water and slowly add 2 cups / 250g of flour (not all the flour!).
  3. Stir well and let the mixture rest until you see bubbles form (this should take only a few minutes: if bubbles do not form, your yeast may be expired).
  4. Add in the oil and the salt.
  5. Add the flour a little at a time, mixing with your hand until the dough forms a smooth ball and does not stick to your hands as much (Do not keep adding flour if you have reached the right consistency, or your dough may be stiff or dry. You may not need to use all the flour).
  6. Knead for about 5 minutes
  7. Oil the bottom of the bowl so the dough does not stick, cover the bowl with a damp kitchen towel and let the dough rise for 2 hours
  8. Remove the dough from the bowl and carefully divide it into 8 equal pieces. Form these pieces into smooth balls
  9. Cover the 8 pieces with the damp kitchen towel and let rest for 30 more minutes
  10. Roll each ball into shape using a floured rolling pin, and optionally let rest 5 more minutes before cooking (for optimal puffyness!)
  11. Heat a pan on medium-high heat (Make sure it is hot before cooking your first Pita, or it may become dry)
  12. When the pan is hot, place the Pita onto it
  13. Flip when one side is charred, and once fully cooked, place aside. Repeat the process with all the other Pita.
  14. It is a good idea to taste the first Pita to make sure it is cooked through.
  15. Enjoy!

Tip!

Never forget the salt in any bread dough recipe!

Always use a very hot pan to cook flatbread, or they dry out.

Pita goes very well with some Falafel, raw veggies and yoghurt sauce.
