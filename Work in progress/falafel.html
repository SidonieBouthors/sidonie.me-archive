Falafels & Pita

Ingredients (Falafels)

  • Chickpeas (530g drained)
  • Onions (5 large)
  • Garlic (2 large cloves)
  • Fresh Parsley/Cilantro (1 large handful)
  • Flour (4 tbsp)
  • Ground Cumin (2 tsp)
  • Ground Coriander Seeds (2tsp)
  • Lemon Juice (2 tbsp)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Ground Black Pepper (to taste)
  • Neutral Frying Oil

Yield: 8 people

Prep Time:

Cooking Time:

Wait Time:

Total Time:

Directions (Falafels)

  • Chop the Onions and chosen Herbs, finely mince the Garlic
  • Add the drained Chickpeas, Herbs, Onion and Garlic to a food processor
  • Blend until you are able to form the mixture into shape (Do not blend too much! This should not be a paste!)
  • Add the Flour, Spices and Lemon Juice and mix well
  • Form into desired shape (spheres or flattened spheres)
  • Add enough Neutral Frying Oil to a cooking pot so it will cover at least half of the Falafels
  • Heat the oil on Medium-High
  • Dip a wooden spoon into it: if bubbles form, it is hot enough (do not heat the Oil too hot! it may burn the Falafels)
  • Gently put the Falafels into the Oil
  • They should cook 3-5 minutes on each side: wait until a crust forms before flipping or they may disintegrate
  • Once the falafel are a dark golden brown color on both sides, remove from the Oil
  • Make sure to drain the Falafels well: you can set them on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess Oil
  • Once all the Falafels are cooked: Enjoy!

Tip!

Flattened spheres will require less oil to cook

Ingredients (Pita Bread)

  • Flour (6 cups or 750g)
  • Olive Oil (3 tbsp)
  • Water (2 cups or 470ml)
  • Dry Yeast (2 packets or 4.5 tsp)
  • Salt (3.5 tsp)
Yield: 8 people
Total Time: 1 hour
Prep Time: 1 hour

Directions (Pita Bread)

  • Warm your water in the microwave (it should be lukewarm to the touch, not too hot)
  • In a bowl, add the yeast to the water and slowly add 2 cups or 250g of Flour (not all the flour!)
  • Stir well and let the mixture rest until you see bubbles form
  • (this should take only a few minutes: if bubbles do not form, your yeast may be expired)
  • Add in the Oil, the Salt, and the rest of the flour little at a time whilst stirring well
  • Once the dough is to stiff to stir with a spoon, knead with your hands
  • You may not need all the flour: add it a little at a time until the dough forms a smooth ball and does not stick to your hands as much
  • (Do not keep adding flour if you have reached the right consistency, or your dough may be stiff or dry)
  • Knead the dough for about 5 minutes
  • Oil the bottom of the bowl so the dough does not stick, and cover the bowl with a damp kitchen towel
  • Let the dough rise for 2 hours
  • Remove the dough from the bowl and place it on a counter
  • Carefully divide it into 16 equal pieces and form these pieces into smooth balls
  • Cover with the damp kitchen towel and let rest for 30 more minutes
  • Roll each ball into shape using a floured rolling pin, and let rest 5 more minutes before cooking
  • Heat a pan on Medium-High heat (Make sure it is hot before cooking your first Pita, or it may be dry)
  • When the pan is hot, place one Pita onto it
  • Flip when one side is charred, and once fully cooked place aside and repeat the process with all the other Pita
  • It is a good idea to taste the first Pita to make sure it is cooked through
  • Enjoy!
